## 1.1 Technical Approach Overview: A Mission-Driven Campaign Engine Built for USSS

The United States Secret Service occupies a singular position in American law enforcement — its agents and officers operate at the intersection of physical courage, technical sophistication, and sustained discretion. Recruiting the right candidates for Special Agent (SA), Uniformed Division (UD), and Administrative/Professional/Technical (APT) positions requires more than advertising; it requires a deep command of the law enforcement workforce market, a disciplined programmatic capability to reach qualified candidates at scale, and the cultural fluency to communicate the USSS mission in a way that resonates with the 21- to 40-year-old LEO, military, and veteran audiences USSS most needs to attract.

Our team brings all three. We have generated more than 300,000 qualified applications for a federal law enforcement agency over a three-year national campaign, driven a major military branch to its first fully achieved recruiting mission in four years, and reversed multi-year declines in sworn officer applications for one of the nation's largest police departments — all through the same integrated, data-driven, multi-channel methodology we propose to deploy on behalf of USSS.

Our technical approach is organized around three interdependent capabilities: **(1) Programmatic Applicant Generation at Scale**, the engine that produces 70,000+ qualified applicants per year through active job boards, programmatic digital advertising, and streaming media; **(2) Full-Spectrum Campaign Execution**, the full-service machine that coordinates recruiting events, ELAC hosting, recruiter training, promotional materials, and branded digital assets while maintaining USSS brand integrity at every touchpoint; and **(3) Intelligence-Driven Campaign Optimization**, the analytics and talent management technology layer that turns raw performance data into actionable decisions — in near real-time — so that USSS leadership always knows where its investment is working and where it needs to be redirected.

The sections below address each requirement in PWS Sections 1.2.1 through 1.2.7 and directly respond to all six evaluation sub-criteria identified in the solicitation.

## 1.2 Advertising and Marketing: Multi-Channel Strategy and Programmatic Applicant Generation (PWS 1.2.1)

### 1.2.1 Programmatic Job Board and Active Job Channel Strategy

The solicitation's most weighted technical sub-criterion is **proficiency in multi-channel recruiting, especially active job boards with programmatic advertising**, and the operationally decisive metric is the ability to reach **70,000+ applicants per year** through those channels. We address this requirement head-on because it is where our approach is most differentiated.

Our programmatic job board strategy treats active job channels not as passive postings but as dynamic, continuously optimized media buys. We maintain active, managed presences on **Indeed, LinkedIn, ClearanceJobs, ZipRecruiter**, and supplemental platforms including **Handshake** (for university and ROTC pipeline) and **Hire Heroes USA** (for transitioning military). Each platform is managed with bid logic tuned to applicant quality — we optimize toward completed applications and qualification thresholds, not raw clicks or impressions.

We demonstrated this methodology at scale during our **TSA National Recruitment Campaign** (October 2022 – September 2025, $24.6M, prime contractor), where we managed active programmatic job board placements for Transportation Security Officer and Federal Air Marshal positions across all 50 states and U.S. territories. Over that three-year period, the campaign drove **more than 300,000 qualified applications** while simultaneously reducing cost-per-qualified-application by **38% from Year 1 to Year 3** — a direct result of continuous bid optimization that shifted spend toward the channels and creative variants producing the highest downstream completion rates. Our G4 Analytics attribution model, described in Section 1.5 below, was the analytical engine behind that cost reduction.

For the USSS NRAC, we propose a tiered active job board structure:

| Platform | Primary USSS Role Target | Key Targeting Parameter |
|---|---|---|
| Indeed Sponsored | SA, UD, APT | Criminal justice, law enforcement, military-adjacent keywords; geographic radius targeting by field office |
| LinkedIn Job Slots + Sponsored | SA, APT | Criminal justice/intelligence/security degree filters; 3–8 years experience; veteran status |
| ClearanceJobs | SA, Intelligence/APT | Active or clearance-eligible candidates; TS/SCI experience |

### 1.2.2 Multi-Channel Media Strategy and Audience Targeting

USSS recruits three distinct position families — Special Agents, Uniformed Division Officers, and APT professionals — each with a meaningfully different demographic profile, motivation structure, and media consumption pattern. Our audience strategy is built around this differentiation.

**Special Agents (Ages 21–37, statutory maximum for initial appointment):** This audience skews toward college-educated individuals with strong interest in criminal justice, national security, intelligence, and military/veteran communities. The most effective media channels for this segment are:

- **Streaming Video (Hulu, Peacock, Paramount+, Connected TV):** Crime, investigative, and national security programming adjacencies, which correlate strongly with SA candidate interest profiles; geo-targeted to metro areas with USSS field offices and to military installation catchment areas
- **YouTube Pre-Roll:** 15- and 30-second non-skippable units in crime drama, news, military history, and fitness content categories
- **LinkedIn Sponsored Content and Sponsored Jobs:** Targeting criminal justice/political science/intelligence degree-holders at the 2–7 year career mark
- **Paid Search (Google/Bing):** High-intent keyword capture for "Secret Service jobs," "federal law enforcement careers," "how to become a Secret Service agent," and related terms
- **Reddit and Podcast Advertising:** True crime, law enforcement, and military veteran podcast networks (e.g., The Last Defense, Cleared Hot) and subreddits (r/ProtectAndServe, r/Veterans) where the SA demographic organically concentrates

**Uniformed Division Officers (Ages 21–40):** The UD candidate is often currently serving in municipal law enforcement, military law enforcement (MP, SP, Marine Corps law enforcement), or corrections, and is seeking advancement in a mission-driven environment. Effective channels include:

- **Streaming Audio (Spotify, Pandora, iHeart):** Commuter dayparts targeting currently employed LEOs who consume audio media during patrol or commuting hours
- **Meta (Facebook/Instagram):** First responder professional groups, police-adjacent Facebook communities, Instagram fitness and tactical gear audiences
- **Out-of-Home and Transit:** Placements near police academies, law enforcement training facilities, military installations, and court/justice complexes
- **Law Enforcement Trade Media:** Police1, Officer.com, and Law Enforcement Today — both display advertising and sponsored content

**APT Positions:** This heterogeneous category includes IT, finance, legal, and administrative professionals. The strategy shifts toward LinkedIn, Indeed, and platform-specific professional association digital advertising (ISACA for cybersecurity, AICPA for finance), with creative messaging anchored on mission impact rather than law enforcement identity.

**Sample Task Order Deliverables — Streaming Media:** The solicitation requires the contractor to deliver **84 million streaming video impressions per year** and **140 million streaming audio impressions per year**. Our media buying infrastructure — built on relationships with major programmatic DSPs including The Trade Desk, Magnite, and direct publisher agreements with Spotify for Government — gives us the scale and buying power to place these impression volumes within USSS-approved audience and content parameters. During the TSA campaign, we deployed comparable streaming video volumes geo-targeted to airport catchment areas, with airport-specific creative variants tied to the same attribution framework described in Section 1.5. We are confident in both our buying capability and our ability to verify delivery through third-party measurement (DoubleVerify or Integral Ad Science).

**Sample Task Order Deliverables — Pre-Roll YouTube:** The solicitation requires **25 million YouTube pre-roll impressions per year**. We manage YouTube campaigns through Google Ads for Government accounts with full access to TrueView, non-skippable, and bumper ad formats. Creative for USSS would be developed in close coordination with USSS's Office of Communications and executed strictly under written USSS Director approval, in compliance with **18 U.S.C. 709**, which prohibits unauthorized use of the Secret Service name or insignia. Our brand compliance process is detailed in Section 1.7.

**Strategic Advertising Plans:** At the outset of each ordering period and no later than 30 days after task order award, our team will deliver a written Strategic Advertising Plan covering: campaign objectives by position category; target audience profiles; recommended channel mix with rationale; proposed creative concepts and messaging framework; media flight schedule with impression/application projections; and research and measurement methodology. The strongest UD and SA candidates are often currently employed in law enforcement or military service — they are not passively browsing job boards. Recruiting events, career fairs, and targeted community engagement reach this population where it already congregates.

Our event recruitment capability is proven. For USSS, our event strategy integrates three tiers:

**Tier 1 — Major Career Fairs and USSS-Branded Recruiting Events:** We will support USSS Recruiting Division (RECDIV) at national career fairs (including federal government job fairs, law enforcement expos, and veteran hiring events such as the Hiring Our Heroes program and DAV Career Fairs). **Tier 2 — Targeted Law Enforcement and Military Community Events:** We will build and maintain a calendar of recruiting events at police academies, law enforcement associations (IACP, NOBLE, NALEO's law enforcement caucuses), military installations and transition assistance programs, and university criminal justice programs. **Tier 3 — Integrated Special Events Campaigns:** When USSS identifies a specific geographic market or demographic target for a hiring push, our team will design integrated campaign packages combining event attendance, hyper-local digital advertising (geo-targeted within a 25-mile radius), social media event promotion, and post-event remarketing. ## 1.8 Innovation in Sourcing: Active and Passive Candidate Strategies

The solicitation evaluates **innovation in sourcing techniques for both active and passive candidates**. Active candidates — those currently searching for a new position — are reachable through job boards, paid search, and career events. Passive candidates — those not actively job-hunting but potentially persuadable — require a different approach.

Our passive candidate strategy for USSS draws on two proven frameworks:

**Awareness-to-Interest Nurture Sequences:** Streaming video, streaming audio, and connected TV advertising functions primarily as awareness-building for passive candidates who are not yet in the labor market for a career change. Our creative strategy for these channels will anchor on the USSS mission — protection of national leadership, financial crime investigation, and elite training standards — rather than on transactional job messaging. A prospective UD Officer who sees a compelling 30-second connected TV spot today may not apply for six months, but that impression establishes the brand consideration that drives the eventual application. We track this dynamic through our G4 attribution model, which credits awareness-stage media for eventual conversions using time-lagged attribution windows.

**Targeted Veteran and Law Enforcement Transition Outreach:** We maintain active engagement relationships with military transition assistance program (TAP) offices, VSOs (VFW, American Legion, DAV, IAVA), and law enforcement association professional networks. For USSS, we will build a structured outreach program specifically targeting:

- Military personnel separating from combat arms, military police, and special operations MOS/ratings — the highest-alignment feeder pipeline for Special Agent positions
- Currently employed local and state LEOs at the 3–8 year mark considering federal advancement — the prime UD pipeline
- Criminal justice, political science, and national security undergraduates in their junior year — the longest-lead but most investment-efficient pipeline

Our Department of State engagement has demonstrated the power of this structured, institution-based passive outreach: by establishing **47 formal partnerships** with MSIs, HBCUs, and HSIs, we grew underrepresented-group applicant share by **31% in the first 18 months** and increased FSOT registrations from partner institutions by **4.2×**. We propose comparable institution-based partnership frameworks for USSS — focused on university criminal justice programs, ROTC programs, and law enforcement academies — to build long-lead SA and UD pipelines that compound in value over the IDIQ period.
