### Project Overview

Cotton Consulting served as prime contractor for the Transportation Security Administration's national recruitment advertising campaign, delivering a fully integrated, multi-channel program to attract qualified applicants for Transportation Security Officer (TSO) and Federal Air Marshal Service (FAMS) positions across all 50 states and U.S. territories. Over a three-year period of performance, our team designed and executed programmatic job board advertising, paid social and paid search, YouTube pre-roll, streaming audio, and out-of-home placements — all instrumented with a custom media-to-application attribution model that connected each creative variant and channel investment directly to downstream applicant outcomes. The campaign drove more than 300,000 qualified applications over the contract term, supporting TSA's sustained hiring requirements during a period of significant traveler volume growth, while simultaneously reducing cost-per-qualified-application by 38 percent and growing the Spanish-language applicant share from 4 percent to 17 percent of the total applicant pool. Cotton Consulting earned an Exceptional CPARS rating in each of the three consecutive performance evaluation periods.

---

### Relevance to This Opportunity

This engagement directly demonstrates Cotton Consulting's proficiency across the core technical requirements of the USSS NRAC solicitation. First, with respect to **multi-channel advertising capability** (PWS Sections 1.3.1 through 1.3.5 and Technical Evaluation Factor I), our TSA campaign simultaneously managed programmatic job boards — including Indeed, ZipRecruiter, LinkedIn, and Clearance Jobs — alongside paid social, paid search, streaming video, streaming audio, and out-of-home placements, a channel architecture that maps directly to the media mix required for the USSS NRAC. Second, with respect to **active job board management and the 70,000+ applicant-per-year requirement** (Technical Evaluation Sub-criterion (d)), our TSA program sustained more than 100,000 qualified applications per year at scale, demonstrating that Cotton Consulting operates comfortably above the applicant volume thresholds specified in the NRAC solicitation. Third, with respect to **analytics, attribution, and pre- and post-advertising research** (PWS Section 1.3.10 and Technical Evaluation Sub-criterion (e)), we built a custom Google Analytics 4-based attribution dashboard for TSA that tied creative variants and media channel investment to applicant flow — precisely the reporting infrastructure the USSS requires under its Google G4 analytics and media tracking requirements. Fourth, this is a **federal Department of Homeland Security client**, meaning Cotton Consulting has direct, firsthand experience operating within DHS compliance requirements, brand standards, and stakeholder dynamics. Fifth, the TSA population — hourly, security-focused, geographically distributed, competing against private-sector logistics and retail wages — presents sourcing and targeting challenges comparable in character (if not in specific job function) to those facing USSS Special Agent and Uniformed Division recruitment, demonstrating our **ability to adapt and scale recruitment strategies** (Technical Evaluation Sub-criterion (f)) for varied federal uniformed workforce audiences.

### Project Overview

Cotton Consulting led a multi-year recruitment acceleration initiative for U.S. Army Recruiting Command (USAREC) at a moment when nearly every U.S. military branch was falling short of accession goals. Our engagement combined the deployment of our proprietary CADENCE™ predictive lead-scoring and recruiter-routing platform across all six Army recruiting brigades, a national multi-channel media program spanning paid social, programmatic advertising, gaming/Twitch, and connected television, and an embedded analytics team that provided real-time mission-readiness intelligence to USAREC headquarters and brigade leadership. We also delivered a comprehensive recruiter enablement program that trained more than 400 Army recruiters on platform use, lead prioritization, and candidate conversion — a train-the-trainer model closely aligned with the USSS NRAC's requirement for annual recruiter sales training. ### Relevance to This Opportunity

This reference speaks most directly to three of the USSS NRAC's most demanding technical requirements. We do not teach generic sales skills; our USAREC curriculum was built specifically for uniformed law enforcement and military recruiting, where the "sale" involves a high-stakes life decision by the candidate and where recruiter credibility and mission understanding are central to conversion. Second, with respect to **innovation in sourcing and the use of predictive analytics to anticipate market shifts** (Technical Evaluation Sub-criteria (b) and (e)), the CADENCE™ platform demonstrates Cotton Consulting's ability to bring enterprise-grade talent intelligence to a federal recruiting mission — identifying where high-propensity candidates are concentrating, dynamically routing recruiter effort, and continuously retraining predictive models based on cohort outcome data. The USSS Special Agent and Uniformed Division hiring pipeline faces structural sourcing challenges — security clearance eligibility, physical fitness requirements, geographic flexibility demands — that are directly analogous to the Army recruiting environment in which we delivered proven results. At $41.2M total contract value, this reference also demonstrates our capacity to manage federal recruitment programs at a scale larger than the anticipated NRAC scope, supporting a **Relevance** rating of highly similar.

## Gaps and Recommendations

### Gap 1: Electronic Talent Management and Tracking Technology (PWS Section 1.3.8 — Yello or Equivalent)

None of the four submitted references explicitly cite deployment of a commercial talent management platform such as Yello for event management, candidate pipeline tracking, or career fair data collection via mobile devices. The USSS solicitation specifically names Yello as an illustrative technology and requires demonstrated capability in this area. **Recommendation:** Before submission, confirm whether the TSA or USAREC engagements included a commercial talent management or event-tracking tool, and if so, name it explicitly in the relevant narrative. If no existing reference captures this, prepare a brief supplemental technology narrative in the Technical Volume that describes Cotton Consulting's experience with Yello, Beamery, Phenom, or a comparable platform. If Cotton has implemented Yello or an equivalent for a current commercial or state/local client not included here, consider whether a non-federal supplemental reference would add value and whether the solicitation's reference instructions permit it.

### Gap 2: Streaming and Broadcast Impression Volume at NRAC-Specified Scale

The solicitation's Sample Task Order specifies highly defined impression thresholds — 84 million streaming video impressions per year, 140 million streaming audio impressions per year, and 25 million pre-roll YouTube impressions per year. While the TSA reference addresses streaming video and audio as delivery channels, our submitted materials do not cite specific annual impression counts for those channels that can be directly compared to these thresholds. **Recommendation:** Prior to submission, confirm with the TSA engagement team whether impression-level data by channel and year is available and disclosable. If TSA-specific figures can be cited (even as approximations or ranges), inserting them into the TSA narrative would directly and measurably address this evaluation dimension. Alternatively, if Cotton's media buying team can document impression delivery from any other engagement, add a specific callout.

### Gap 3: Quality Control Plan Experience

The solicitation requires a QCP within 15 business days of award. None of the submitted narratives include a specific reference to a formal Quality Control Plan developed and maintained on a comparable contract. **Recommendation:** Add a sentence to the TSA or USAREC narratives — or to the Technical Volume — confirming that Cotton Consulting has maintained a formal QCP on those contracts and describing the structure (review cadence, corrective action protocol, stakeholder reporting). This is a low-effort addition that removes an evaluator uncertainty.

### Gap 4: Direct Mail and Print Production

The PWS lists direct mail and print as required advertising channels. None of the submitted references specifically call out direct mail production or management. **Recommendation:** Confirm with the TSA or LAPD engagement teams whether direct mail was executed as part of those campaigns. If so, add a single bullet point to the relevant Key Accomplishments section. If not, address print and direct mail capability in the Technical Volume with a brief description of Cotton's production management process.

### Gap 5: Federal Law Enforcement Audience Specificity

While the LAPD reference provides the strongest LEO recruiting credibility in the portfolio, it is a state/local engagement rather than a federal one. The TSA and USAREC references are federal but involve uniformed aviation security and military accession rather than Title 18 federal law enforcement. No submitted reference covers a federal LEO agency recruiting campaign (e.g., FBI, DEA, CBP, ATF, or another component of DHS). **Recommendation:** If Cotton Consulting holds any current or recent subcontract relationship, task order, or advisory engagement with a federal LEO agency's HR or recruiting function that has not been submitted here, evaluate whether it can be added as a fifth reference. Even a smaller-dollar advisory engagement demonstrating familiarity with federal LEO hiring standards, background investigation requirements, and OPM classification frameworks would meaningfully strengthen Sub-criterion (c). If no such reference exists, ensure the Technical Volume narrative explicitly discusses Cotton's understanding of Title 5 and Title 10 LEO hiring processes, USSS suitability requirements, and the USSS RECDIV organizational structure.
