1.0 BACKGROUND

The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) administers the nation's major 
healthcare programs, serving over 160 million Americans. CMS collects, processes, 
and analyzes vast quantities of healthcare data to support program integrity, policy 
development, quality measurement, and beneficiary services.

CMS currently operates multiple legacy data analytics platforms that were developed 
independently across program offices. These platforms use varying technology stacks, 
have limited interoperability, and lack modern machine learning and artificial 
intelligence (AI) capabilities. The agency seeks to modernize its data analytics 
infrastructure and build enterprise AI capabilities that can be leveraged across 
the organization.

This procurement replaces the existing Enterprise Analytics Support contract 
(Contract No. 75FCMC-20-D-0003) currently held by Accenture Federal Services, 
which expires September 30, 2026.

2.0 SCOPE OF WORK

The contractor shall provide comprehensive data analytics, data engineering, and 
artificial intelligence support services to CMS, including but not limited to:

2.1 Data Platform Modernization
- Migrate existing analytics workloads from on-premises infrastructure to CMS Cloud 
  (AWS GovCloud) environments
- Design and implement a unified data lakehouse architecture using modern tools 
  (e.g., Databricks, Snowflake, or equivalent)
- Establish data governance frameworks including metadata management, data quality 
  monitoring, and lineage tracking
- Implement role-based access controls and ensure compliance with HIPAA, FedRAMP High, 
  and CMS Acceptable Risk Safeguards (ARS)

2.2 Advanced Analytics and AI/ML
- Develop and deploy machine learning models for fraud, waste, and abuse (FWA) 
  detection across Medicare and Medicaid claims data
- Build natural language processing (NLP) pipelines for automated review of 
  clinical documentation, appeals, and provider correspondence
- Create predictive models for beneficiary risk stratification and care management
- Implement responsible AI practices consistent with OMB M-24-10 and the 
  NIST AI Risk Management Framework
- Evaluate and pilot large language model (LLM) applications for internal CMS 
  use cases, including policy research assistance and regulatory document analysis

2.3 Data Visualization and Reporting
- Develop executive dashboards and self-service reporting tools using Tableau, 
  Power BI, or equivalent platforms
- Create automated reporting pipelines for congressionally mandated reports
- Support ad hoc data requests from CMS leadership, OIG, and congressional offices

2.4 Data Engineering and Operations
- Maintain and optimize existing ETL/ELT pipelines processing over 2 billion 
  claims records annually
- Implement DataOps practices including CI/CD for data pipelines, automated 
  testing, and monitoring
- Provide 24/7 production support for mission-critical analytics systems with 
  a 99.9% uptime SLA

5.0 EVALUATION CRITERIA

This acquisition will be evaluated using a Best Value Tradeoff methodology. 
Proposals will be evaluated against the following factors, listed in 
descending order of importance:

Factor 1: Technical Approach (Most Important)
- Understanding of CMS data environment and healthcare analytics challenges
- Proposed architecture for data platform modernization
- AI/ML methodology and responsible AI practices
- Innovation in approach to LLM integration

Factor 2: Management Approach (Important)
- Program management methodology and risk mitigation
- Staffing plan and key personnel qualifications
- Quality assurance and performance monitoring approach
- Transition plan from incumbent contractor

Factor 3: Past Performance (Important)
- Relevant experience with federal healthcare data analytics
- Demonstrated ability to deliver AI/ML solutions at scale
- References from federal healthcare agencies
- Note: Past Performance and Management Approach are approximately 
  equal in importance

Factor 4: Price (Less important than non-price factors combined)
- Proposed labor rates by labor category
- Rate escalation methodology for option years
- Price reasonableness and realism analysis

The Government will assess the overall best value by considering the 
relative merits of non-price factors against price. The Government may 
award to other than the lowest-priced offeror if the non-price factors 
justify the price premium.
